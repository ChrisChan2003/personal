About Chris

“I have hated words and I have loved them, and I hope I have made them right.” That is a quote from my favorite book The Book Thief by Markus Zusak. When I first read these words in my high school English classroom, I had no idea how much I would think back on them. Now, as an aspiring technical writer and recent English graduate of the University of Massachusetts Amherst, I approach writing with that same mindset: making complex information clear, meaningful, and accessible, to make words right and give a voice to the voiceless.

Technology & Communication

My background in English, combined with my interests in technology and psychology, has shaped the way I think about technical communication. I graduated with a BA in English and minors in IT and psychology.

I like challenging myself and learning new software. To me, overcoming the learning curves that come with picking up new technology is exciting. Whether I’m working with documentation platforms like MadCap Flare, coding in HTML/CSS and Python, or exploring accessibility and UX writing principles, I’m motivated by the challenge of turning complex ideas into intuitive experiences.

Beyond the Profession

Outside of professional work, I’m passionate about video games and creative projects. I enjoy the process of building skills over time and seeing steady progress take shape — something that influences both the way I learn and the way I work.
